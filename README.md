## Overview
Dual-Mode Halo v3 is a comprehensive, modular concept for creating an artificial mini-magnetosphere around Mars using a Starlink-inspired swarm of laser-linked microsats equipped with high-temperature superconducting (REBCO) coils. The system operates in **dual modes**:
- **Magnetic Shielding Mode**: Deflects >90% of galactic cosmic rays (GCRs) and solar energetic particles (SEPs) for crew protection during transit and surface operations.
- **Particle Dispersal Mode**: Releases high-albedo ice/Al-coated microspheres for controlled sublimation, inducing precipitation cycles, temperature regulation, and albedo forcing to accelerate terraforming.

The design leverages existing SpaceX technologies (Starship payload delivery, Starlink laser interlinks, REBCO development for Raptor nozzles) for low-cost, scalable deployment (<$50M development, <$5M per unit at fleet scale).

## Key Features
- **L1 Swarm Dipole Configuration**: Dynamic coordination forms a planetary-scale magnetosphere at Mars-Sun L1, retaining atmosphere and enabling pressure buildup above the Armstrong limit.
- **Precipitation & Temperature Control**: Sublimation-induced cloud seeding creates rain cycles with soil-fertilizing particles, hydrating regolith for plant growth.
- **Phased Bio-Terraforming**: Introduction of extremophiles (earthworms, resilient insects, cyanobacteria) followed by hardy plants and pollinators, leading to self-sustaining ecosystems.

- **Economic Advantages**: Synergies with Starship v3 and Starlink reduce marginal costs; cheaper per sievert protected than passive shielding.
